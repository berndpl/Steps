2026-07-05 · visits + flyover

The places you go, and a 3D walk to close the gap

Steps learned where the steps could come from. An opt-in visit tracker uses Apple's low-power CLVisit dwell detection to log the places you linger — no polling, no route trace, just arrivals and departures — and a History sheet lists them with the real round-trip walking cost home ↔ there: distance from MapKit's MKDirections, reverse-geocoded place names, and an estimated step count from your average stride. When a round trip would close today's gap to 10,000, the main view says so plainly — "A round trip to the café would get you there." e63894c

Tap that nudge and it takes flight. A WalkFlyoverView fetches the real walking route, builds the there-and-back path, and drives a camera along it over photorealistic 3D imagery (.hybrid(elevation: .realistic)). MapKit has no "fly a route" API, so the tour is made scrubbable by baking the path into a KeyframeTimeline<FlyPose> once — position follows the route, heading follows the smoothed path — then evaluating it at any time so a progress slider (and a play/pause loop) can run the walk back and forth. Tilt and zoom are held constant, dialled with two sliders, and the framing you pick is remembered for next time.

2026-06-29 · inbox + icon

An inbox, a log, and a new face

Steps grew an Inbox — one place that gathers the day's notices instead of letting them flash and vanish: the nudges, milestones, and badges now land in a list you can scroll back through, with a notification log behind them. The look stays in step across surfaces too, with a small theme sync so the gold and green follow your palette choice everywhere. fa26709

The app also got a new face: a hand-built Icon Composer icon replaced the placeholder set — two pink dots over a dotted dark grid, a quiet nod to the contribution grid inside. Signing moved to a gitignored Local.xcconfig so the team ID stays out of the repo, and the accessory widgets switched to a ProgressView ring (not a Gauge) and stopped showing stale step counts. 56bae34

2026-06-28 · watch

A wrist, a chime, and little badges for the rest of the day

Steps reached the wrist. A small watchOS app shows today against the goal as a ring, and the same Steps Ring and Tiny Steps views that drive the lock screen now appear as watch-face complications — one set of code, three surfaces. The watch reads HealthKit on its own, since the App Group cache doesn't cross devices. 7209f44

The grid learned to notice the rest of the day. If you logged a ride, a meditation, or a strength session, a little badge sits beside today's count — cycling, mind-and-body, or strength-training. That meant asking Health for workouts and mindful minutes too, including for people who'd already granted steps. 865b9e7

And the alerts got more fun, with their own voice. Beyond the per-thousand nudges there's a morning greeting, a double-goal cheer, goal-streak milestones, and a record-day shout — each at most once a day. They all ring a custom bell bundled with the app rather than the system default, and today's screen now shows cycling minutes when there's a ride to show. 38252dc

2026-06-28 · grid

The grid, dialled in

The single spread slider became a proper instrument. A row of curve shapes — linear, ease-in, ease-out, ease-in-out, log, exp — each shown as a tiny sparkline, decides how a day's steps map to fill, with a strength slider for how hard. The palette shelf grew from five to twelve, and the month's-best day can wear a dot, ring, asterisk, star, or nothing. 4a2b84e

Today got easier to find: its ring is now a brightened version of the palette's goal colour, stroked just outside the cell so the fill underneath stays whole and it never blends into a goal-day. 2dcd685

2026-06-27 · widgets

Off the home screen, onto the lock screen — and a grid you can tune

The widget stopped waiting for the app to open. An HKObserverQuery with HealthKit background delivery now refreshes it when your steps actually change — instantly in the foreground, roughly hourly when closed — and the timeline heartbeat tightened from an hour to fifteen minutes. Then it left the home screen entirely: two lock-screen accessories, a Steps Ring (count plus a goal gauge) and Tiny Steps (a single SF Symbol that evolves every 1,000 steps), built tint-safe so they read on the desaturated lock screen where the gold and green can't go. 0eaebcb

Notifications arrived, opt-in: one catch-up nudge per crossed thousand, a louder one at the 10k goal, and "New monthly best!" the moment today beats every other day this month — a challenge that resets with the calendar. They ride the same step observer, so they cost nothing extra in the background.

The contribution grid grew a dial. It's generated continuously in OKLCH now instead of five fixed buckets, so a day's exact steps get an exact colour; a response-curve spread slider decides how hard goal days pop while the middling days recede. A paintbrush sheet opens palette presets, full-custom ramp and goal colour wells, and a day-shape pick (rounded, circle, squircle), all previewed on a live example month. The chosen goal colour ripples out to become the app accent, the month's best day wears a quiet dot, and every choice is written to the App Group so the widget wears it too. 0eaebcb

2026-06-25 · design

Two surfaces, one gold thread

The app and the widget stopped sharing a look. The app now wears notes-plontsch — Catppuccin Mocha (dark) / Latte (light) surfaces, monospaced SF Mono throughout, flat, with weight flattened so emphasis comes from size and colour, not bold. The row of debug chips along the bottom collapsed into a single ladybug menu in the corner. d32e768

The widget went the other way — the Spark/Letters widget family: a near-transparent backdrop, a container-relative shape, 18pt insets, a steps://open deep-link, and the footer trimmed to just the gold number, nothing else. What keeps them one app is the gold. It's the accent, it's the goal-reached cell, and now it's today's ring too — promoted from a neutral stroke to the same gold so the whole thing speaks one colour. b9199c2

2026-06-25 · design + widget

A gold identity, today's count, and a ring on today

Steps got its own look. Applying the Deskgym style (SF Rounded bold monospacedDigit numbers, a single Liquid-Glass .glassProminent action, flat surfaces with no shadows) but with a deliberately distinct accent: gold #F5A623. The gold doubles as the grid's goal-reached colour, so a day that clears 10,000 steps flips from the darkest green to the same gold as the app's number — one coherent identity instead of a borrowed one.

The widget also stopped being grid-only. A small footer now sits under the month — a figure.walk glyph, today's total in gold, and a muted "today" — and today's cell carries a Color.primary ring so you can find it at a glance against any fill, green or gold. The grid and footer are one shared StepsMonthView, so the home screen and the in-app preview can't drift apart.

Provenance note: the earlier post said Steps skips an App Group. That changed — a SharedStore in the App Group container now lets the app sync recent totals for the widget to read instantly, with the direct HealthKit query kept only as a first-run fallback. The old decision is left below as written, because that's how it actually went.

2026-06-24 · widget

A month of steps as a contribution grid

The home-screen widget lays out the current calendar month like a real calendar — seven weekday columns across, week rows down, the 1st sitting in its true weekday slot and honoring whether your week starts Sunday or Monday. Days outside the month stay blank so the month's shape reads at a glance; future days this month render as faint placeholders.

Each day's colour is bucketed against a fixed 10,000-step goal in quarters: empty, then four greens lifted from GitHub's contribution ramp (#9be9a8 → #216e39). The goal is fixed on purpose — a green square should mean the same thing every day, not drift with a moving target. The grid is a single StepsGridView shared between the app and the widget, so the in-app preview and the home screen render from the exact same code. 62638e6

2026-06-24 · architecture

Reading Health without an App Group

App and widget both need step data, and the textbook path is an App Group with a shared cache to hand totals from one to the other. Steps skips it. HealthKitService.swift is compiled into both targets, and HealthKit authorization granted to the app is inherited by its extensions — so the widget queries HKStatisticsCollectionQuery directly, with no shared container and nothing to keep in sync.

The app itself stays a single live view that routes through four phases — loading, needs-permission, ready, denied — driven by what HealthKit will tell us (it deliberately hides whether read access was granted). A DEBUG-only preview switcher, compiled out of release builds, lets any of those screens open with one tap during development without seeding data. 62638e6

Decided for

  • One HealthKitService.swift, a member of both targets
  • Widget queries HealthKit directly — the extension inherits the app's read auth
  • Re-query on each refresh; nothing persisted, always fresh

Decided against

  • An App Group container to pass data app → widget
  • A cached or synced step store to keep the two in step
  • A configurable goal, which would make the colour scale ambiguous
